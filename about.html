About Me

I am currently a trainee Web Developer with Netmatters and have been engaging with the Scion Bootcamp since November last year. Born into the latter part of Generation X I grew up alongside the evolution of was peaked by home computer's such as BBC Microcomputers and the ZX Spectrum (armed with, respectively, a whopping 32k and 48K of RAM). It wasn’t until I started GSCE Computer Science (in the early 90’s) that I started touching code but by then the evolution of technology was already taking shape and I started to see a move away from loading programmes via tape cassette and a switch to floppy disks on computers such as IBM Personal Computers, Atari ST and Commodore Amiga home computers. Interestingly the switch away from BBC microcomputers to IBM machines happened exactly halfway though my Computer Science studies and to this day I can still conjure up the image of our tutor standing with the teacher notes open explaining that he did not why our programs didn’t work. Of course in those days the internet was not accessible to mere mortals and if the right books were not in the library then a retail adventure would begin.

Having finished school I found myself drawn away from studying technology and I became more interested in social issues such as economics, politics and social issues. I then went on to study Social Sciences at University, a broad topic, which saw me undertake modules in quantitive research methods (which was fairly hardcore maths) and management information systems (which again was about computer’s and covered the news of the day “The Millenium Bug” and data protection).

Post finishing University I worked for a short time as a Finance Assistant, where I received training in Excel, Access, various bespoke internal systems and was involved in developing a management information system to assist with the implementation of a new Financial Management System. After 2 years, having found there was little other development potential, I decided to train to become a Probation Officer. A capacity in which I worked for over 15 years, gaining very specific soft skills, assessment and analysis techniques. However, after this amount of years I found I was no longer able to challenge myself within the Criminal Justice System and looked further afield, completing a self-study level 4 diploma in Accounting in 2020. Having struggled to present an effective career changing strategy to employers I eventually decided to work for myself and was able to help numerous businesses maintain their compliance with International Accounting and Financial Reporting Standards. Accounting is though where my interest in coding again started to peak, as through increasing my Excel skills (using IF statements, data formatting and conditional logic) I began to become more interested in the technical side.

To take my interest further, after the worst of the pandemic had struck, I enrolled on a full stack web development course, where I gained a German based distance learning qualification (ZFU) in Web Development. The advice and guidance of the mentored career module then saw me gain employment in the IT sector with QA Consulting.

Whilst I was keen to further the web development avenue, with QA, I was placed on a DevOps training pathway. This pathway saw me learn about Python, CI/CD pipelines, databases, cloud technologies, containers and configuration management. During the latter part of the course I also experienced a repeat of my GSCE day’s, where again some of the technical problems we faced were not resolvable by the main tutor. However, I and a few others, would not be defeated and after many Google searches and collaboration I ended up as one of the few people on the pathway to have a functioning project. Disaster was though to later befall my role, as a turn in economic conditions meant myself and pretty much all of my colleagues were not deployed to client sites. So, instead I focused on the companies upskilling requirements, gaining numerous internal certificates in technologies such as Terraform, Docker, Ansible and Kubernetes. I was also able to complete the official software testing pathway (gaining a Foundation level ISTQB certificate in Software Testing) and the AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner Certificate. When the employment with QA come to end I then gained a place on the Netmatters Scion course, where I continue to develop technical skills and expertise in the field of programming the Web.
